# Definition P ≡ Q
Propositions P and Q are logically equivalent if they produce the same truth value

### Example
Show that p → q is logically equivalent to ¬p V q

| p | q | ¬p | p → q | ¬p V q |
|---|---|----|----|--------|
| T | T | F | **T** | **T** | 
| T | F | F | **F** | **F** | 
| F | T | T | **T** | **T** | 
| F | F | T | **T** | **T** |

Propositions _p → q_ and _¬p V q_ produce the same truth values!

∴ p → q ≡ ¬p V q
